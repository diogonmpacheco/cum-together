# Alpha Playbook

## Purpose

Run closed alpha sessions that answer product questions, not just gather vague reactions.

## Current Alpha Shape

- closed alpha only
- public room is still gated by an access phrase
- link rooms are invite-only and carry an access token in the URL
- automatic local room bridge works for same-origin testing
- manual P2P fallback remains for direct WebRTC debugging
- one live remote peer is real today; the rest of the room is equal-seat prototype context
- no voice, no chat, no DMs, no contact exchange

## What To Test

1. Does the intro make the product legible in under a minute?
2. Does no-face setup feel protective instead of shame-based?
3. Does paused entry reduce pressure?
4. Does witness mode feel safe or extractive?
5. Do host controls feel calm and obvious?
6. Do people understand the room is closed alpha and intentionally scoped?

## Session Formats

### 1 host + 1 guest

Use this for:
- invite flow
- waiting room admission
- automatic bridge
- pause, hide, leave, and remove

### 1 host + 1 witness

Use this for:
- witness expectations
- host witness policy
- whether witness mode feels acceptable to visible participants

### 1 host + small observed group

Use this for:
- intro comprehension
- whether the room feels private, adult, and non-competitive
- whether the interface feels too large, too dense, or too abstract

## Session Script

1. Send the full invite link or public alpha phrase.
2. Ask the participant to narrate what they think this product is before joining.
3. Watch whether they understand:
   - closed alpha
   - no-face
   - silent room
   - no contact exchange
4. Have them choose visible, paused, or witness entry.
5. Admit them into the room.
6. Test:
   - pause and resume
   - report flow
   - witness policy
   - host lock and remove
7. End with a short interview.

## Questions To Ask

- What did you think this room was before you joined?
- Did anything feel unsafe or unclear before entry?
- Did no-face framing feel natural or stressful?
- Would you choose visible, paused, or witness by default?
- Did the room feel collective or lonely?
- What would make you stay longer?
- What would make you leave immediately?
- If you are a woman, queer user, older user, fat user, disabled user, or shy user: what still feels missing for safety or dignity?

## Success Criteria

- participants understand the product promise quickly
- no-face setup feels normal
- paused entry is used and appreciated
- host admission and removal are understood
- witness mode does not feel predatory
- nobody asks for chat, DMs, or follower mechanics as essential to the core room

## Fail Signals

- participants think it is a cam site or dating app
- the product feels performative instead of protective
- people do not understand why silence matters
- witness mode feels extractive
- users want to exchange contact because the room itself feels too thin
- host controls feel hidden or authoritarian

## Notes Discipline

Log each session with:

- date
- room type
- participant mode
- what confused them first
- what they trusted first
- what made them stay
- what made them hesitate
- one design fix
- one policy fix
- one technical fix
